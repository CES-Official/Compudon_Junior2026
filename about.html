About COMPUDON Junior Championship

COMPUDON Junior Season VI is now an International Championship for junior school students. It was launched with inspiration of Microsoft's Digital Literacy Standard, which teaches generic ICT skills and concepts. Now, the championship curriculum also covers essential content to help participants stay safe online, gain an overview of new technologies and also acquire skills of media literacy.

The Championship will test school students' age-appropriate ICT skills that are vital for success in the 21st Century. There are three levels to this school-focused championship. The top three winning positions from eligible participating countries will be invited to compete in the International Finals round. In the International Finals, these students will represent their school and their country, competing to win prestigious awards. Over 100 awards are available.

COMPUDON Junior is open to school students between the ages of 8-14 years. Preparing for the championship will not only help school students acquire and master the critical 21st century skills but also prepare them to thrive and take advantage of the opportunities being created in the 21st-century global economy. The practice exams provided will help students understand the type of questions, the time available, and prepare them to excel in the championship.

COMPUDON Junior is brought to you by CyberLearning Educational Society (CES), which is also the organizer of COMPUDON (WWW.COMPUDON.IN) – now amongst India’s most popular student championships. COMPUDON is India’s first-ever Official Championship on MS Office Skills, which tests students’ skill levels on MS Word, MS Excel & MS PowerPoint through an online, real-time, hands-on, skill-based exam.

About CyberLearning Educational Society (CES)

CES is the NGO arm of India CyberLearning Pvt Ltd., a Global Training Partner and Training Services Partner of Microsoft. It is a group of committed individuals who are problem-solvers and leaders, dedicated to creating lasting change and empowering and securing every child through digital literacy for success in the 21st-century global economy.

For more than 17 years, CES has contributed to the cause of digital literacy through partnerships with over 50 Rotary Clubs in Rotary International District 3010 (which later divided into Districts 3011 & 3012), Times Foundation, Huawei, Microsoft and Intel. CES has supported youth from minority and underprivileged sections with training and global certification scholarships worth 2 crores.

CES is also the organizer of COMPUDON, India’s first ever-Official Championship on Microsoft Office Specialist Certifications and the International Championship on Digital Literacy- WWW.COMPUDONJUNIOR.IN
